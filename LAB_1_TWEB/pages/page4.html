☣ The Classics — Where It All Began

Fixed cameras. Limited ink ribbons. Typewriters that felt like salvation.
The games that defined an entire genre and haunted a generation.

Resident Evil (1996)

The Spencer Mansion. The first zombie. The first dog.
A masterclass in atmosphere built from pre-rendered backgrounds and tank controls.
"Jill, here's a lock pick. It might be useful if you, the master of unlocking, take it with you."

Resident Evil 2 (1998)

Raccoon City falls. Leon and Claire arrive on the worst possible night.
Two campaigns. One city. Mr. X relentlessly hunting you through the RPD.
Perhaps the most beloved entry in the entire series.

☣ The Reinventions — Genre Redefined

Over-the-shoulder cameras. Mob enemies. Action blended with tension.
The games that risked everything and won.

Resident Evil 4 (2005)

Leon sent to rural Spain. Found a cult. Found Ashley Graham. Found his destiny.
Revolutionized third-person shooters and the entire action genre.
"Your right hand comes off?"

Resident Evil 7: Biohazard (2017)

First-person. Louisiana bayou. The Baker family dinner.
A complete reinvention that returned the series to pure, intimate terror.
VR compatible — the bravest checkbox in gaming history.
