☣ The Complete Timeline

From a haunted mansion in the Arklay Mountains to a crumbling Romanian castle — the Resident Evil series has spanned nearly three decades of survival horror. Every mainline game, remake, spin-off, and film in the order they were unleashed upon the world.

// Timeline Index
  • 1990s — The Origin of the Nightmare
  • 2000s — Expansion and Revolution
  • 2010s — Reinvention
  • 2020s — The Modern Era

1990s — The Origin of the Nightmare

1996
[ Mainline #1 ]

Resident Evil

The Spencer Mansion. The first zombie. The first dog through the window. Chris Redfield and Jill Valentine investigate S.T.A.R.S. Bravo team's disappearance and discover Umbrella's T-Virus experiments buried beneath the estate. The game that launched survival horror as a genre.

Platforms: PlayStation · Sega Saturn · PC · Director's Cut (1997)
1998
[ Mainline #2 ]

Resident Evil 2

Raccoon City falls into a sea of the undead. Leon Kennedy's first day on the job and Claire Redfield's search for her brother converge at the Raccoon Police Department. Two campaigns, one city, Mr. X relentlessly hunting you through the halls. Perhaps the most beloved entry in the entire series.

Platforms: PlayStation · Nintendo 64 · PC · GameCube (2003)
1999
[ Mainline #3 ]

Resident Evil 3: Nemesis

Set before and during RE2's events. Jill Valentine attempts to escape Raccoon City while a relentless bioweapon — Nemesis — hunts her through the burning streets. Introduced dynamic encounters, live selection choices, and the most iconic monster the series ever produced.

Platforms: PlayStation · PC · Dreamcast

2000s — Expansion and Revolution

2000
[ Mainline #4 ]

Resident Evil — Code: Veronica

Claire is captured by Umbrella and imprisoned on Rockfort Island. The first game in the series with real-time 3D backgrounds. Introduces Alfred and Alexia Ashford, and reunites Claire with Chris in the Antarctic finale.

Platforms: Dreamcast · PS2 (X, 2001) · GameCube · PS3 / Xbox 360
2002
[ Remake ]

Resident Evil REmake

A ground-up rebuild of the 1996 original for Nintendo GameCube. New environments, expanded story, Lisa Trevor, Crimson Heads — fans consider it the definitive version of RE1.

Platforms: GameCube · PC · PS3 / Xbox 360 · PS4 / Xbox One (2015)
2002
[ Mainline Prequel ]

Resident Evil Zero

A prequel set the night before S.T.A.R.S. arrives at the mansion. Rebecca Chambers and escaped convict Billy Coen fight through a stranded Umbrella training facility train. Introduced the partner-swap mechanic.

Platforms: GameCube · Wii · PC · PS3 / Xbox 360
2002
[ Film #1 ]

Resident Evil (2002) — Film

The first Anderson film. Alice awakens with amnesia in the Hive, an underground Umbrella facility beneath Raccoon City. Directed by Paul W.S. Anderson. Starring Milla Jovovich. Earned $102M worldwide.

Theatrical · DVD / Blu-ray
2003
[ Spin-off ]

Resident Evil Outbreak

Online co-op survival set during the Raccoon City outbreak. Eight civilian survivors, each with unique abilities, fight through scenarios across the city. Ahead of its time; still beloved for its atmosphere and tense item management.

Platforms: PlayStation 2 (Online)
2004
[ Film #2 ]

Resident Evil: Apocalypse — Film

Alice escapes Raccoon City as it falls. Introduces Jill Valentine and Carlos Oliveira in live-action. Nemesis makes his theatrical debut. The city is nuked at the end.

Theatrical · DVD / Blu-ray
2005
[ Mainline #5 ]

Resident Evil 4

Leon Kennedy travels to rural Spain to rescue the President's daughter from Los Illuminados. Revolutionized the third-person shooter genre with over-the-shoulder aiming and a merchant system. Arguably the most influential game ever made.

Platforms: GameCube · PS2 · Wii · PC · HD (2011) · Remake (2023)

2010s — Reinvention

2012
[ Spin-off ]

Resident Evil: Revelations

Originally on Nintendo 3DS. Jill Valentine investigates the cruise ship Queen Zenobia, battling mutant Ooze creatures. A deliberate return to atmosphere and tension after RE5's action focus.

Platforms: 3DS · PS3 / Xbox 360 / PC / Wii U · PS4 / Xbox One
2012
[ Mainline #7 ]

Resident Evil 6

The most ambitious and most divisive entry. Four interwoven campaigns: Leon, Chris, Jake Muller (Wesker's son), and Ada Wong. Global bioterrorism on an unprecedented scale.

Platforms: PS3 · Xbox 360 · PC · PS4 / Xbox One (2016)
2017
[ Mainline #8 ]

Resident Evil 7: Biohazard

A complete reinvention. First-person perspective, Louisiana bayou, the Baker family. Ethan Winters searches for his missing wife Mia and finds something far worse than he imagined. Fully playable in PlayStation VR.

Platforms: PS4 · Xbox One · PC · Nintendo Switch (Cloud)
2019
[ Remake ]

Resident Evil 2 (Remake)

A ground-up reconstruction using the RE Engine. Mr. X redesigned as a map-wide stalker who cannot be permanently stopped. Won numerous Game of the Year awards.

Platforms: PS4 · Xbox One · PC · PS5 / Xbox Series

2020s — The Modern Era

2020
[ Remake ]

Resident Evil 3 (Remake)

Jill Valentine's escape from Raccoon City rebuilt in the RE Engine. Nemesis is faster, smarter, and can pursue you through previously safe rooms. Bundled with Resident Evil: Resistance — an asymmetric online mode.

Platforms: PS4 · Xbox One · PC
2021
[ Mainline #9 ]

Resident Evil Village

Ethan Winters travels to a snowbound Eastern European village to recover his abducted daughter Rose. Ruled by Lady Dimitrescu and three other lords — the series' most iconic new character in years.

Platforms: PS4 / PS5 · Xbox One / Series · PC · Nintendo Switch (Cloud)
2023
[ Remake ]

Resident Evil 4 (Remake)

Capcom's most ambitious remake. RE4's entire campaign reconstructed with RE Engine fidelity. Includes Separate Ways DLC with Ada Wong's full perspective. Topped most 2023 Game of the Year lists.

Platforms: PS4 / PS5 · Xbox Series · PC
2026
[ Mainline #10 ]

Resident Evil Requiem

The tenth mainline Resident Evil game. Features FBI analyst Grace Ashcroft, who investigates a series of mysterious deaths involving survivors of the Raccoon City incident, alongside returning protagonist Leon S. Kennedy.

Platforms: PS5 · Xbox Series · PC
